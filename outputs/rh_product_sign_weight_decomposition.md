# Product Sign-Weight Decomposition

The previous scans treated

```text
A_Y(r) = sum_{ab=r, Y<=a,b<2Y} mu(a)mu(b)
```

as the main product coefficient sequence. This note separates its sign from its
positive divisor-splitting weight.

If `mu(a)` and `mu(b)` are nonzero, then `a` and `b` are squarefree. For a valid
split `ab=r`, every prime exponent in `r` is either `1` or `2`.

For each prime:

```text
exponent 1 in r: the prime is in exactly one of a,b
exponent 2 in r: the prime is in both a,b
```

Therefore

```text
mu(a)mu(b) = (-1)^(omega(a)+omega(b)) = (-1)^Omega(r).
```

This sign is independent of the particular split `ab=r`. Hence, on the support
of the product coefficients,

```text
A_Y(r) = lambda(r) W_Y(r),
```

where

```text
lambda(r) = (-1)^Omega(r)
W_Y(r) = #{(a,b): ab=r, Y<=a,b<2Y, mu(a)mu(b) != 0}.
```

## Lesson

The next analytic target can be stated more concretely:

```text
lambda(r) W_Y(r)
```

has strong local autocorrelation cancellation on log-product shells, and the
dominant source of that cancellation already appears in the Liouville-type sign
`lambda(r) = (-1)^Omega(r)`.

The proof route should now focus on a local autocorrelation estimate for
`lambda(r)` on the product set generated by squarefree dyadic factors, with
`W_Y(r)` treated as a slowly varying divisor-splitting weight.
